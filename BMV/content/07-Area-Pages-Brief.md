# Area Pages Brief — Shaftesbury, Wincanton, Sturminster Newton

Follows the **area-page recipe** proven on Marley Moves and First Taxis:
8 required elements, 700-1000 words (design-surface-dependent, not a
hard number), gated by the **"remove location name" test** — delete the
town name from the draft; if a reader still can't tell which town it's
about from the streets/landmarks/access notes alone, rewrite it.

Structure mirrors the actual live Marley Moves Shaftesbury page (fetched
and analysed for this brief): trust strip once → hero → "why here"
positioning → routes/access/parking → property types with real street
names → practical tips → FAQ accordion → nearby areas cross-link. Adapt
"property types" the way First Taxis adapted it for its domain — for a
plumbing/heating business, period-property heating quirks are directly
relevant (unlike taxis, this translates almost unchanged from Marley's
removals framing).

**Sourcing discipline (non-negotiable):** every street, landmark and
fact below is verified against a primary source (Wikipedia,
Historic England, or the live Marley/First Taxis research already done
for these exact towns). Anything marked `[VERIFY]` needs a final check
(Royal Mail/OS Maps for streets, postcodes.io for postcodes, an actual
route planner for road-miles) before publish — do not guess past what's
sourced here. If Rob supplies a genuine operational anecdote for a
town (a specific job, a specific street's access quirk), that becomes
the required "earned detail" element — richer than anything sourced
from a wiki page, and the single most important line on the page.

**Landmarks (all Wikipedia-verified):** Sturminster Newton Mill (a mill
has existed on the site since at least 1016; resumed commercial flour
production in 2020), St Mary's Church (rebuilt 1486 by the abbots of
Glastonbury Abbey), the Town Bridge / Rolls Bridge (a six-arch stone
bridge dating to around 1500, widened from 12 to 18 feet in 1820, with
a surviving 15th-century notice threatening transportation for anyone
who damages it), the Market Cross (15th century, market history on the
site dating to at least 1219), Sturminster Newton Castle (a 14th-century
manor house site), The Exchange arts centre (opened 2007 on the former
cattle-market site). **Population:** 4,408 (2021 census). **Literary
connection:** Thomas Hardy lived in the town 1876-1878 and wrote *The
Return of the Native* there.

## Common structure for all three pages

1. Trust strip (once, not repeated inside body copy).
2. Hero + H1: "Plumbing and Gas Services in [Town]".
3. "Why we cover [Town]" — the intro paragraph above.
4. "Routes and access" — the access note.
5. "Property types we work on" — the property-types row, using real
   street/area names.
6. Earned detail block — only ships once Rob supplies a real story.
7. Service cards (all 4 services, linking to the dedicated pages).
8. FAQ accordion (the 4-6 location-specific questions above) +
   matching `FAQPage` schema.
9. RelatedAreas (the other 2 towns) + RelatedServices (all 4) +
   RelatedPosts (3 most recent Insights/Guides).

### Schema per page
`areaServiceSchema({ name, slug, region, description })` +
`faqPageSchema(location-specific faqs)` + `breadcrumbListSchema` (from
the visible breadcrumb component only).
